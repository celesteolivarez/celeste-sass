Band Biography

Biography:

Harvey Dale and the Cements was born in the misty streets of Portland, Oregon, in 2015 when childhood friends Alex Monroe (vocals/guitar) and Sam Carter (drums) reconnected over their shared love for haunting melodies and atmospheric soundscapes. The duo soon recruited bassist Riley Thompson and synth wizard Jules Moreno, completing their lineup and setting the stage for a musical journey that would blur the lines between alternative rock, dream pop, and indie aesthetics. With a sound reminiscent of bands like The National, Beach House, and Slowdive, Harvey Dale and the Cements quickly gained a following in the Pacific Northwest's underground music scene. Their debut EP, Fading Lights (2017), was a moody exploration of love, loss, and the fleeting nature of time, earning them critical praise for their ethereal sound and emotionally charged lyrics. Their first full-length album, City of Reveries (2019), showcased a matured, more expansive sound, blending reverb-drenched guitars with hypnotic synth lines. The record's lead single, "Neon Horizons," became a surprise indie hit, receiving heavy rotation on college radio and streaming platforms. Harvey Dale and the Cements’ reputation for immersive live performances—marked by atmospheric lighting, hypnotic visuals, and an electrifying stage presence—helped them embark on their first national tour in 2020. Despite the challenges of the pandemic, the band adapted by hosting intimate virtual performances and collaborating with other artists to release Echoes in Isolation (2021), a collection of stripped-down reworks and new material inspired by the solitude of lockdown. Returning to the studio with a reinvigorated spirit, the band released Fragments of Midnight in 2023, an album that delved deeper into themes of self-discovery, nostalgia, and resilience. The record cemented their status as one of the most compelling alternative acts of the decade, featuring cinematic production and deeply personal songwriting that resonated with fans worldwide. As they continue to push musical boundaries, Harvey Dale and the Cements remains a beacon of emotion and artistry in the indie music landscape, captivating listeners with their ability to turn everyday moments into sonic daydreams. Whether performing under the neon haze of a packed venue or crafting their next mesmerizing record, Harvey Dale and the Cements proves that the night is always alive with possibility.
